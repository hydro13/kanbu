# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone who participates in the Kanbu project and community will be treated with respect and dignity, free from harassment and discrimination.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment:

- Being respectful and constructive in communication
- - Welcoming differing opinions and experiences
  - - Focusing on what is best for the community
    - - Showing empathy towards other community members
      - - Using inclusive language and avoiding assumptions
        - - Respecting confidentiality and privacy of others
         
          - Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
         
          - - Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination based on any protected characteristic
            - - Offensive comments related to gender, sexual orientation, race, religion, disability, or any other characteristic
              - - Personal attacks or insults
                - - Unwanted sexual attention or advances
                  - - Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
                    - - Publishing others' private information without consent
                      - - Deliberately disruptive behavior
                        - - Threats of violence or harm
                         
                          - ## Our Responsibilities
                         
                          - Project maintainers are responsible for:
                         
                          - - Clarifying what behavior is expected and acceptable
                            - - Taking appropriate action in response to violations
                              - - Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
                                - - Banning contributors who behave in unacceptable ways
                                 
                                  - ## Scope
                                 
                                  - This Code of Conduct applies to:
                                 
                                  - - All spaces managed by the Kanbu project (code, issues, discussions, wiki, etc.)
                                    - - Official project communication channels
                                      - - Public spaces where project members act as representatives
                                        - - Any other interactions where project members identify as Kanbu contributors
                                         
                                          - ## Enforcement
                                         
                                          - Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by:
                                         
                                          - - Emailing: conduct@kanbu.dev or R.Waslander@gmail.com
                                            - - Reporting via GitHub's built-in tools
                                              - - Direct message to a project maintainer
                                               
                                                - All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality of the reporter.
                                               
                                                - ## Consequences
                                               
                                                - Violations may result in:
                                               
                                                - 1. **Warning**: A private message addressing the concern
                                                  2. 2. **Temporary Suspension**: Removal from participation for a specified period
                                                     3. 3. **Permanent Ban**: Indefinite removal from the project community
                                                       
                                                        4. The severity of consequences depends on the nature and severity of the violation, and repeated violations will result in escalation.
